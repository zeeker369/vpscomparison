## Pricing Overview

DigitalOcean pricing is simple and transparent.

### Basic Droplets

- $4/month — 1GB RAM
- Flat monthly pricing
- No hidden NAT gateway fees
- Predictable bandwidth allocation

Compared to AWS, cost estimation is significantly easier.

### What You Typically Pay For

- Droplets (compute)
- Managed databases (optional)
- Block storage
- Data transfer beyond included limits

For small to mid-scale projects, pricing remains straightforward.

### Compute

- Fast SSD-backed Droplets
- Simple scaling
- Predictable performance tiers

### Storage

- Block storage volumes
- Managed databases
- Spaces (S3-compatible object storage)

### Networking

- Private networking
- Load balancers
- Floating IPs

DigitalOcean favors usability over advanced networking complexity.

## Pros & Cons

### Pros

- Extremely simple UI
- Predictable monthly pricing
- Fast server provisioning
- Excellent for WordPress, SaaS MVPs, APIs
- Generous new-user credit program

### Cons

- Smaller service ecosystem than AWS
- Fewer enterprise compliance certifications
- Not ideal for very large multi-region systems

## FAQ

### Is DigitalOcean cheaper than AWS?
For small and mid-sized projects, yes — especially because pricing is predictable and avoids many hidden networking costs.

### Is DigitalOcean good for WordPress?
Yes. It’s one of the most popular choices for WordPress hosting among developers.

### Does DigitalOcean offer managed databases?
Yes — including PostgreSQL, MySQL, Redis, and more.

### Is DigitalOcean suitable for enterprise workloads?
It can handle moderate scale, but very large enterprise systems may prefer AWS or Azure.
