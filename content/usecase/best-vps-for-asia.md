## What is the best VPS region for Asia traffic?

For Asia-first workloads, **Tokyo and Singapore** remain the most stable and balanced VPS regions in 2026.  

- **Tokyo** – strong Northeast Asia coverage (Japan, Korea, parts of China)
- **Singapore** – strong Southeast Asia hub (Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand)
- **Seoul** – optimized for Korea-dominant audiences
- **Mumbai** – preferred for India-focused traffic

The best region depends on where your majority users are located.

---

## Regional strategy breakdown

### Tokyo
Best for Japan, Korea and mixed North Asia audiences.  
Typical RTT inside Asia: 20–80ms depending on distance.

### Singapore
Best regional hub for Southeast Asia.  
Strong submarine cable connectivity and stable routing.

### Seoul
Low latency inside Korea.  
Often higher cross-border latency than Tokyo.

### Mumbai
Best choice for India-first platforms.  
Avoid if your audience is outside South Asia.

---

## Practical deployment checklist

1. Test latency from at least **3 real user ISPs**
2. Record average RTT + p95 latency
3. Monitor packet loss and jitter
4. Add CDN for static-heavy workloads
5. Keep database in the same region as your app server

---

## Use case examples

- SaaS serving multi-country Asia audience → Singapore
- Japan-focused startup → Tokyo
- India-based fintech → Mumbai
- Korea-only gaming backend → Seoul

---

## Final recommendation

If unsure, start with **Tokyo or Singapore**, then optimize based on real traffic data.  
Asia routing quality matters more than raw geographic distance.
